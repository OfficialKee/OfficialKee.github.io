Orlando

Orlando (/ɔːrˈlændoʊ/ or-LAN-doh) is a city in, and the county seat of Orange County, Florida, United States

Orlando, pronounced /ɔːrˈlændoʊ/ or-LAN-doh, serves as both a city within and the county seat of Orange County, Florida, in the United States. Positioned in Central Florida, it serves as the focal point of the Orlando metropolitan area, which boasted a population of 2,509,831 residents, as reported by the U.S. Census Bureau in 2017. This region ranks as the sixth-largest metropolitan area in the Southern United States and the third-largest in Florida, trailing behind Miami and Tampa Bay. As of the 2020 census, Orlando's city population stood at 307,573, positioning it as the fourth-largest city in Florida, following Jacksonville, Miami, and Tampa. Notably, it holds the distinction of being the largest inland city in the state.


Orlando stands out as one of the globe's top destinations, drawing crowds mainly for tourism, significant events, and conventions. In 2018 alone, it welcomed over 75 million visitors, solidifying its popularity. Notably, Orlando International Airport ranks as the 13th busiest in the US and the 29th busiest worldwide. The city boasts two iconic attractions: the renowned Walt Disney World Resort, established by the Walt Disney Company in 1971, situated approximately 21 miles southwest of downtown Orlando in Bay Lake, and the Universal Orlando Resort, which debuted in 1990 as a significant expansion of Universal Studios Florida and remains the sole theme park within Orlando's city limits.
